Drifts used in building a Commando
by Fred Eaton

Last updated 10/31/05

Over the years I have made a number of drifts to remove and install bearings, bushings, oil seals and other like items. Since I am building a Commando from the frame up and documenting every step, I decided to label and dimension all the drifts that I use. Some day when I get my turning center, I may produce sets of drifts for sale and rent, until then if you have access to a lathe, you can make some of the drifts needed to build a Commando. Most people use a socket as a drift and you should also be able to find different sockets that fit the dimensions listed below. The advantage of a drift over a socket for inserting a seal or bearing is that the first step will center the drift on the seal or bearing. If you do use a socket, make sure your socket is centered and will not hang up on the bore.

This list is not complete and will grow as I build this Commando.

The above diagram shows what I mean by First Step & Second Step. A few drifts have a third step for convenience, but if the third step is important, it will be listed in the Drift Use section. Please note that for installing a bearing, the First Step will fit into the inner race of the bearing and there should be a recess on the face of the Second Step, so that step only presses against the outer race and does not touch the inner race.
